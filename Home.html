Verteporfin

What is Verteporfin?

Verteporfin is a medication primarily used in photodynamic therapy to treat certain eye conditions, such as macular degeneration. Research suggests that verteporfin may have potential applications in scar healing by modulating cell signaling pathways involved in tissue repair and regeneration.

Description

On April 23rd, 2021, Dr. Michael Longaker, Stanford’s vice chair of surgery, and Geoffrey Gurtner, Stanford’s vice president of surgery for innovation, published a paper in Science titled “Preventing Engrailed-1 activation in fibroblasts yields wound regeneration without scarring.” This revolutionary research made it to the NYTimes. In mice they made 6 mm in diameter circular full thickness excisions, these wounds were then injected with Verteporfin and they healed to the point that verteporfin-healed skin was nearly indistinguishable from normal skin, with recovery of skin appendages, ultrastructure, and mechanical strength. This was confirmed via histological analysis and machine learning algorithms.

Description

On July 22nd, 2022, Dr. Barghouthi tried using verteporfin on a “test” FUE on his website. He set up 3 control and 3 test locations across the donor region, where the control was injected with PBS and the test locations were injected with verteporfin with a concentration of 2 mg/mL, which was the concentration used in pigs, with dosages of 0.24 mg/cm^2, 0.32 mg/cm2, and 0.4 mg/cm2. These dosages were extrapolated from the mice study, as the dosages for the pig study haven’t been released.

Description

On July 11th, 2023, Dr. Bloxham trialed verteporfin on 3 FUT patients on his YouTube channel. These patients were unique; different skin-types, different histories (some had prior surgery, some did not), and different amounts of tissue were taken and tested. Some had sections of scar removed, treated with verteporfin and closed again; others had virgin tissue removed, treated with verteporfin, and closed. Differing concentrations (based upon past studies) of the medication were used as well. The study is still ongoing but results already appear promising, with more neogenesis of hair follicles being present in the treated regions, as well as healthier looking skin.
